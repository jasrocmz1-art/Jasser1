1. Introduction

This report details the practical workshop sessions conducted on Tuesday (2025/11/11) and Wednesday (2025/11/12). The primary objective was to apply theoretical knowledge of workshop safety, hand tools, and power tools to a practical project.

"The task assigned to our group (DataVolt Hvac-5) was to fabricate a wooden square box, requiring precision in measuring, cutting, assembling, and finishing."

2. Workshop Safety & PPE

Safety is the top priority. The following equipment was mandatory.

Safety Helmet

Protection against falling objects in the workshop.

Safety Goggles

Crucial for eye protection while sawing or hammering.

Blue Coveralls

"EWA" standard uniform to protect clothing and skin.

HyFlex Gloves

Essential grip and protection against wooden splinters.

3. Tools & Materials

Hand Tools

  • Claw Hammer: Used for driving nails and removing bent ones.
  • Try Square: Essential for ensuring 90-degree corners.
  • Measuring Tape: "Measure twice, cut once."
  • Files & Sanding Block: For smoothing rough edges.

Power Tools

  • Power Saw / Jigsaw: Used for cutting larger wooden planks efficiently.
  • Power Drill: For creating pilot holes to prevent wood splitting.
  • Power Sander: Speeding up surface finishing.

4. The Fabrication Process

Day 1: Tuesday (2025/11/11)

Preparation & Cutting

We interpreted the dimensions, marked cut lines using try squares, and cut the base and side panels ensuring straight edges.

Day 2: Wednesday (2025/11/12)

Assembly & Finishing

We assembled the box using nails and teamwork. One student held the alignment while another hammered. Finally, we sanded the box and labeled it.

Conclusion

The workshop was a success, resulting in a sturdy, well-constructed wooden box. The guidance provided by Mr. Abdulaziz Alshehri was instrumental. We gained confidence in handling tools, reinforced our safety habits, and improved our teamwork skills.

Key Takeaway: Precision and Safety are the pillars of good craftsmanship.
